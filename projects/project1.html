Project Overview

I designed and fabricated a functional Electric Piano using scrap wood, 3D-printed components, and an Arduino microcontroller. The wooden frame and lever system provided structural support, while custom CAD-modeled keys were 3D printed for consistent geometry. Button triggers mounted beneath each key served as inputs, and sound output was generated through a piezo buzzer controlled by the Arduino.

Engineering Challenges & Solutions

Precision in Fabrication

Challenge: Accurate measurements were critical to ensure uniform key spacing and reliable lever action.
Solution: Refined blueprinting process and learned to take consistently accurate measurements.

Polyphony (Multiple Notes at Once)

Challenge: Initial attempts limited the piano to playing a single note at a time.
Solution: Implemented the Arduino “Mozzi” audio synthesis library to enable multiple tones to be played simultaneously.

Counterweight Calibration

Challenge: Implementing weighted keys was a fine balnce between too heavy or light.
Solution: Designed experiments to test counterweights, iterating until optimal balance was found.

False Triggering

Challenge: Mechanical weight and software errors caused unwanted activations.
Solution: Combined calibrated counterweights with refined code logic for reliability.
