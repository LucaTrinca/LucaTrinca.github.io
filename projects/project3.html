Project Overview

As part of my APSC 101 course, I designed and built an automated retrieval device that competed in a series of engineering challenges. The frame and claw mechanism were fabricated from sheet metal, requiring precise cutting, bending, and assembly. To guide the design, my team and I developed detailed technical drawings and models of the system.

My primary role was programming the Arduino-based control system. Using ultrasonic sensors, I developed reliable code to automate the retrieval process, ensuring consistent detection and actuation of the claw mechanism. During the final competition, the system operated flawlessly, contributing to our team placing in the top 10 overall.
